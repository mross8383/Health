Methylation — The Hub of the Wheel

Methylation is Gary Brecka's central concept. It is a sequence of biochemical conversions in the body — where one raw material becomes the waste product that feeds the next process. Many common conditions that appear fixed and unresolvable are actually downstream consequences of broken methylation caused by nutrient deficiencies.

The Methylation Chain — From Green Leaves to Sleep

The Tree Analogy — Why Treating Symptoms Is the Wrong Approach

A true arborist seeing a rotting palm leaf will not touch the leaf — they will test the soil, find the missing nitrogen, and add it. The leaf heals on its own. Modern medicine focuses on the leaf (the symptom). Brecka's approach focuses on the soil (the nutrient deficiency).

  • You could supplement every trending product — NMN, resveratrol, CoQ10, ashwagandha, vitamin C — and if the core deficiency isn't addressed, nothing fundamental changes
  • Most people supplement for the sake of supplementing because they have no data on their body
  • The solution: get data first, identify deficiencies, supplement specifically for those gaps

The MTHFR Gene — The Most Common Mutation in the World

44% of the global population carries this gene mutation — making it the most common genetic SNP (single nucleotide polymorphism) in the world. It is a simple inability to convert folic acid into its usable form, methylfolate.

  • Folic acid from food or standard supplements cannot be properly converted to methylfolate
  • Methylfolate deficiency is linked to: neural tube defects, mental illness, depression, bipolar disorder, OCD, ADHD, anxiety, severe gut issues, sleep disorders, manic depression
  • It is not folic acid that prevents neural tube defects — it is methylfolate that does this. People with the MTHFR mutation taking standard folic acid are not getting the protection they think they are.
  • The fix is simple and inexpensive: supplement with methylfolate (the pre-converted form) rather than folic acid
  • You cannot fix the gene but you can supplement for its function
This is a test you do once in a lifetime. It tells you exactly what raw materials your body can convert and what it cannot — giving you a precise supplementation roadmap rather than guesswork.

Anxiety, ADD/ADHD & the COMT Gene

Anxiety without a specific trigger is often not a psychological problem — it is a physiological one. Catecholamines (adrenaline, noradrenaline, dopamine) are building up in the body faster than the COMT gene can break them down, creating a chronic low-level fight-or-flight state.

What Is Actually Happening in Anxiety

  • Catecholamines are neurotransmitters responsible for the fight-or-flight response: adrenaline, noradrenaline, a-fedone, dopamine
  • The brain cannot distinguish between a real fear (knife at your throat) and an imagined fear (thinking about sharks in bed) — both activate the same catecholamine response
  • People with slow COMT cannot break these catecholamines down quickly enough — they accumulate, keeping the person in a permanent state of low-level alertness
  • This creates anxiousness, racing mind at bedtime, worst-case-scenario thinking, and an over-reaction to situations that others dismiss as minor
  • They have often had this on and off throughout their entire life — even as a child, feeling fearful or anxious without being able to explain why
  • Anti-anxiety medications typically do not work well for this type — they feel "like a zombie" but the underlying catecholamine issue is not addressed
Hallmarks of slow COMTBody tired but mind awake at night · goes to worst case scenario · feels anxious without a clear trigger · overreacts to situations proportionally · has felt this since childhood · anti-anxiety drugs don't really help The fixMethylated B vitamin complex — specifically methylfolate and methylcobalamin (B12). These provide the raw material to break down catecholamines. Not pharmaceutical suppression — nutritional restoration.

ADD/ADHD — Attention Overload, Not Attention Deficit

Brecka reframes ADD and ADHD entirely. People with these conditions do not lack the ability to pay attention — they lack the ability to pay attention to just one thing. It is an overactive mind, not a deficient one.

  • Adderall and amphetamines work by stimulating the central nervous system to "match the pace of the mind" — but they do not address the underlying cause
  • The goal should be quieting an overactive mind through neurotransmitter balance, not stimulating the CNS to keep up with it
  • Methylated B vitamins provide the raw material for the brain to regulate its own pace, without pharmaceutical CNS stimulation

Essential Biomarkers — What to Test

Most people have more data on their business than on their own body. A basic blood panel answering three questions — blood sugar regulation, hormone balance, and nutrient deficiencies — gives a roadmap that most doctors don't order together.

1. Glycemic Profile — Blood Sugar Regulation

"Blood sugar is the root of all evil." Brecka's most urgent biomarker category. When insulin rises chronically, there is a downstream cascade affecting hormone production, fat metabolism, cardiovascular risk, and triglycerides.

GlucoseAmount of sugar in the blood right now HbA1cThree-month average of blood sugar — shows the trend, not just today's snapshot InsulinThe ratio of insulin to glucose reveals insulin resistance. The higher the insulin needed to control glucose, the more resistant you are. An early warning sign of metabolic syndrome that standard panels miss.
  • High insulin blocks fatty acid metabolism — people with high insulin almost always have high triglycerides (high blood fat), which is a separate cardiovascular risk factor
  • Pre-diabetes is a pandemic — not only caused by sugary foods; high glycemic carbohydrates (white flour, white rice, white bread, white pasta) overpower the pancreas even without obvious sugar intake
  • By addressing insulin alone, you create positive downstream effects across cardiovascular risk, triglycerides, and inflammation

2. Hormone Panel

DHEARaw material the body uses to manufacture hormones including testosterone. Deficiency = hormone deficiency even without an endocrine disorder. SHBGSex hormone binding globulin — a protein that binds testosterone and prevents it from being used as free testosterone. High SHBG = low effective testosterone even if total T looks normal. Free testosteroneThe usable form. Many men have normal total testosterone but low free testosterone because of high SHBG. This is the number that matters. Thyroid panelFull thyroid — not just TSH. T3, T4, and reverse T3 paint a complete picture.
In younger men, low testosterone is often not an endocrine system failure — it is a nutrient deficiency. DHEA and vitamin D3 alone can restore testosterone to the normal range by giving the body the raw materials to manufacture it. This is preferable to exogenous testosterone which shuts down the body's own production.

The deficiency should be tested before a rheumatoid diagnosis is accepted.

Homocysteine & Blood Pressure

85% of hypertension diagnoses are "idiopathic" — of unknown origin. Brecka believes a significant proportion are caused by elevated homocysteine (an inflammatory amino acid) which causes the vascular system to constrict, raising pressure in a fixed system.

  • Elevated homocysteine → vascular constriction → raised blood pressure. Nothing wrong with the heart — the pipe is simply narrower.
  • Fix: supplement with trimethylglycine (TMG / betaine) to break down homocysteine into methionine
  • When homocysteine drops, blood pressure normalises — without pharmaceutical intervention in many cases
  • 2018 study (Journal of Headaches and Face Pain, n=8,819): direct inverse relationship between sodium intake and migraine headaches — as sodium rose, migraines fell. Low sodium (from over-filtering water, sweating without remineralising) can drive the osmotic changes that stretch the dura and cause migraines.

Red Light Therapy & Photobiomodulation

Photobiomodulation (PBM) is the technical term for red and near-infrared light therapy. It is one of the most studied areas in regenerative medicine with over 1,000 published clinical trials. It works by entering the mitochondria and forcing oxygen in — making cells 16× more energy-efficient.

How It Works — The Mitochondria Mechanism

  • Inside each mitochondrion is a motor called the Krebs cycle. Each revolution produces either 2 units or 36 units of ATP (energy). The difference: whether oxygen is present.
  • Red light penetrates the mitochondrial wall and expels mitochondrial nitric oxide — forcing oxygen to dock into the cycle
  • Result: 16× more energy produced per cycle. Cells can now eliminate waste, detoxify, repair, and regenerate at dramatically higher rates.
  • Measurable in real time: saliva nitric oxide strips show elevated nitric oxide levels within 10 minutes of exiting a red light bed
  • Additional benefits: reduces inflammation, increases microvascular circulation, promotes collagen/elastin production, improves angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation)
  • Search term for clinical studies: "photobiomodulation" + your condition of interest (dementia, Alzheimer's, inflammation, skin, etc.)

Longevity Insights from Life Insurance Data

Brecka spent nearly 20 years reading medical records and predicting mortality for life insurance companies. This gave him access to one of the largest mortality databases in the world — covering date, time, location, and cause of death for millions of lives across decades.

What He Saw in the Data

  • Comorbidities are not additive — hypertension + diabetes + obesity is not 1+1+1=3, it is 1+1+1=10. Conditions compound each other massively.
  • He never saw a single centenarian at time of death who did not have clinically elevated LDL cholesterol — raising the question of whether high LDL is a longevity marker rather than a pure risk factor
  • Statins reduce LDL but also reduce the body's ability to produce hormones, cell walls, and cell membranes — trading one risk for others downstream
  • Corticosteroids initially reduce inflammation but damage joints over ~6 years — the data was accurate enough to predict joint replacements and schedule them in advance
  • Simple nutrient deficiencies (D3, B12, DHEA) would routinely present as conditions being treated with powerful pharmaceuticals, accelerating mortality
  • Isolation cuts life expectancy in half at any age. "Broken heart syndrome" (surviving spouse dying shortly after the death of a long-term companion) is a well-documented mortality factor.

Blue Zones — What They Actually Share

The diets of Blue Zones are wildly different from each other — making diet an unlikely common cause of their longevity. Singapore (longest life expectancy) eats the most meat. Sardinia eats high amounts of bread, pasta, and flour. Mediterranean zones eat high fats, oils, and fish.

  • The consistent common thread: mobility into older ages and a sense of purpose
  • No such thing as assisted living in these communities — elderly relatives moved in with family and remained purposeful until death
  • Mortality accelerates post-retirement — correlated with the loss of purpose and reduced community interaction
  • Community has a biological effect all the way down to the cellular level — cells exist in communities too, exchanging waste, detoxifying, and repairing in relation to each other

Isolation & Community — The Data Is Stark

  • Isolation cuts life expectancy in half — at any age. This was used as a comorbidity factor in mortality predictions.
  • Studying cells in isolation (in vitro) and then assuming they will behave the same way inside the body is one of the biggest flaws in biological research — cells in the body exist in communities that change how they behave
  • Loneliness is a comorbidity factor on the same order as smoking, obesity, or diabetes in terms of mortality impact
Community, purpose, and mobility are the common threads of longevity across all Blue Zones — regardless of wildly different diets. These are modifiable factors available to everyone.

GLP-1 Drugs (Ozempic, Mounjaro) — Brecka's View

Brecka's view broadly aligns with other experts — these drugs have legitimate medical applications but have become drugs for vanity, and their side effect profile is being underestimated.

  • Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) and tirzepatide (Mounjaro) — GLP-1 receptor agonists. Tirzepatide outperformed semaglutide in head-to-head trials.
  • Appropriate for: type 2 diabetics, morbidly obese with significant cravings, severe insulin resistance
  • Concerns: gastric emptying slows → paralytic gut → gut contents can putrefy. "Ozempic face" — fat mobilised from cheeks and eye pads, causing sunken appearance.
  • Up to half of weight lost may be lean body mass in some studies — muscle and bone, not just fat
  • If using these drugs, Brecka strongly recommends: a resistance training programme AND a growth hormone peptide (e.g. sermorelin/ipamorelin) to protect muscle mass
  • "For vanity purposes, there are a lot better peptides and better ways to do it"
