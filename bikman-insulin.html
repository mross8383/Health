Insulin — The Master Hormone

Insulin is described as "the one metabolic hormone to rule them all." It tells every single cell in the body — from brain to bone — what to do with energy. Without insulin, it is physically impossible to get fat. With chronically elevated insulin, fat loss is nearly impossible.

High Insulin → Fat Storage Cascade

What Insulin Does — Beyond Blood Sugar

  • Controls energy use in every single cell — brain, bone, liver, lung, fat
  • Slows the body's metabolic rate in order to store more energy
  • Directs calories from the blood into fat cells and the liver
  • Blocks the liver from making ketones — preventing the brain's preferred alternative fuel
  • A low-carb meal vs. a high-carb meal at identical calorie counts produces a measurably higher metabolic rate for hours — because insulin stays lower
Without insulin: physically impossible to get fat. With chronically elevated insulin: fat loss is extremely difficult regardless of calorie restriction.

Insulin Resistance — What It Is & Why It Matters

If you have gained weight, you have insulin resistance. It is impossible to have one without the other. Insulin resistance makes every calorie you eat more likely to be stored as fat.

  • Three things rapidly cause insulin resistance: stress, poor sleep, and excessive carbohydrate consumption
  • Morning insulin resistance is natural (due to cortisol) — do not eat starchy or sugary foods in the morning as it doubles the existing glucose load
  • Alcohol causes insulin resistance — raises cortisol the next day, making every calorie eaten more efficiently stored as fat
  • The HOMA score (using fasting insulin + fasting glucose) is the best accessible measure of insulin resistance
Fasting insulin target≤7 microunits per millilitre — ideal range How to testDoctor blood test — insulin is not routinely measured but should be requested. HOMA score calculated from insulin + glucose together.
Insulin is not included in standard blood panels. Dr. Bikman's single policy wish: add insulin to all routine blood tests globally. Request it specifically from your GP.

Ketones & Ketosis

Ketones are produced by the liver when insulin is low and fat burning is high. They are both a fuel and a signalling molecule — acting like a hormone as well as an energy source. The main ketone is beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB).

Low Insulin → Fat Burning → Ketone Production

Benefits of Ketones (BHB)

  • Brain's preferred fuel — stable, consistent energy vs. the glucose roller coaster
  • Anxiolytic — directly reduces anxiety by signalling in the brain
  • Improves depression and mood
  • Improves attention, memory, and cognition
  • Evidence of benefit in: schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, depression, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, multiple sclerosis, epilepsy, migraines
  • Heart: ketones increase ejection fraction (amount of blood pumped per beat) — L-BHB specifically caused a 40% increase in a pig heart model via vessel expansion
  • Fat tissue: ketones increase the metabolic rate of fat cells by 3× — proven in human biopsies
  • Appetite suppression — brain senses abundant fuel, hunger signals drop
  • Eliminates cravings — brain nourished by stable ketone energy, indifference to sugar emerges
  • Blood pressure: L-BHB increases nitric oxide, expanding blood vessels — Dr. Bikman reports personal blood pressure drop from 139/90 to 110/70 within an hour of L-BHB supplementation

Exogenous Ketones — Forms & Uses

Exogenous ketones let you raise BHB without being in ketosis. They help with the transition into a ketogenic diet and have independent benefits.

Exogenous ketones will temporarily suppress fat burning (lipolysis). The body senses ketones are present and reduces its own fat mobilisation. Use for cognitive/performance benefit, not during fasting windows aimed at fat loss.

Fat Cells & Weight Loss

Weight loss is about shrinking fat cells — not destroying them. Liposuction removes fat cells but zero health markers improve. You must shrink fat cells through hormonal means, not just calorie restriction.

  • Fat cells store energy as triglycerides. In a low-insulin state, triglycerides break down into free fatty acids (lipolysis) and travel to the liver
  • Two variables control fat cell size: calorie quantity AND insulin level. Focusing only on calories without addressing insulin causes hunger — and hunger always wins
  • In ketosis, fat tissue metabolic rate increases 3× — proven in human biopsies. The body becomes a far more efficient fat burner
  • Ketones breathed out or urinated out represent calories wasted — free energy loss that doesn't require exercise
  • Subcutaneous fat (pinchable) burns readily and quickly. Visceral fat (around organs) also responds well to low-insulin states
Every diet works until you stop doing it. A ketogenic diet's advantage is it doesn't rely on hunger suppression through willpower — it suppresses hunger hormonally by keeping the brain fuelled with ketones.

Fatty Liver

The liver is described as "the soccer mom of nutrient metabolism — it knows what to do with everything." When insulin is high, the liver is directed to store fat. When insulin drops, the liver is forced to burn fat and cannot store it.

  • High insulin tells the liver to store incoming fat. Low insulin makes fat storage in the liver impossible — it must burn the fat instead
  • Human studies: people with confirmed fatty liver disease on ultrasound went on a low-carb/ketogenic diet for one week — liver fat reduced by approximately 60%
  • This reduction was significantly greater than the high-carb group eating the same total calories — further evidence that insulin, not calories, is the key variable
  • The liver burns so much fat in ketosis that it makes ketones as a way of sharing the energy surplus with the brain (which cannot burn fat directly)
Fatty liver can be dramatically reversed in as little as one week of low-carb eating — the liver cannot hold onto fat when insulin is low.

Creatine — Deep Dive

Creatine is the first thing Dr. Bikman takes every morning. Its muscle benefits are well established — its brain benefits are the exciting new frontier.

  • Mechanism: creatine stores as creatine phosphate in cells, rapidly recycling ATP (energy currency) — allows more work in less time, shorter rest periods
  • Increases muscle strength by up to 10%, power output up to 15%, reduces muscle damage 20–40% after intense exercise
  • Stimulates myogenic genes — promotes muscle protein synthesis and growth
  • Brain: at 10–15g/day, saturates the brain (5g is insufficient for this). Improves cognition in people with cognitive decline or early dementia
  • Sleep deprivation: human study — sleep-deprived people taking creatine performed significantly better on cognitive tests than sleep-deprived placebo group
  • Alzheimer's: women are affected at almost double or triple the rate of men. Dr. Bikman recommends middle-aged women take creatine daily at the higher dose specifically for brain protection

B Vitamins — A Caution

Animal ranching studies found that adding B vitamin complexes to livestock feed could triple the efficiency with which animals gained weight per pound of food eaten. Dr. Bikman raises this as a concern for humans.

  • B vitamins make mitochondria more efficient at storing energy — which is the opposite of what you want for fat loss
  • For fat loss, metabolic inefficiency is actually desirable — burning more than you store
  • High-dose niacin (a B vitamin) in clinical trials is associated with weight gain
  • Modern fortified foods (especially flour) are heavily loaded with B vitamins — processed food eaters may be getting far more than needed
  • You are unlikely to overload on B vitamins from whole foods like steak and eggs — the concern is with fortified packaged foods
Dr. Bikman's concern: high B vitamin intake may be one of the contributing factors to the obesity epidemic — by making the body too efficient at storing energy from food. Avoid mega-dosing B vitamin supplements unless clinically indicated.

Ashwagandha — Detail

Recommended for people whose gut health or weight issues are rooted in chronic stress and poor sleep — stress is one of three cardinal causes of rapid insulin resistance.

  • Adaptogen — helps the body manage the cortisol stress response
  • Shown in human studies to improve sleep latency (time to fall asleep) and sleep architecture
  • Likely mechanism: suppression of cortisol response
  • Available in gummy form at full therapeutic doses — more palatable than the bitter powder

Foods, Diet & Strategy

Dr. Bikman's approach centres on insulin control rather than calorie counting. The recommended strategy is ketogenic or low-carb, but any dietary approach that lowers insulin will produce results.

Core Dietary Principles

  • Protein and fat have little to no effect on insulin — eat as much as desired
  • Fat has zero insulin effect. Protein has a modest effect depending on context
  • Carbohydrates are the primary insulin driver — controlling carbs controls insulin
  • Fruit and vegetables are acceptable — whole form, not juiced
  • Zero to minimal carbs for breakfast and lunch — morning insulin resistance means starchy foods are particularly harmful in the morning
  • Carbs, if eaten, are best reserved for dinner — when insulin sensitivity is improved and the social/family meal is the priority
  • Big hearty lunch of protein and fat is the key meal — makes evening cravings far more manageable
Stop snacking in the evening. If there is one single change anyone can make, it is this. Evening is when willpower is lowest and metabolic timing is worst — going to bed with a glucose spike activates the sympathetic nervous system and ruins sleep quality.

Keto — Evidence & Concerns

  • Practical advantage: not based on hunger — sustainable long term
  • Takes approximately 16 hours of no eating/low carb before ketosis begins
  • "Keto flu" during transition — exogenous ketones can help ease this
  • People adopt keto lifelong for epilepsy, migraines, and metabolic diseases
  • Not the only way to lose weight — but described as the most practical and simplest
Important exception: People with insulinoma (insulin-secreting pancreatic tumour) must NOT adopt a low-carb diet — they cannot stop producing insulin and will become severely hypoglycaemic. Anyone who feels extremely unwell within hours of starting keto should seek medical advice immediately.

Natural GLP-1 Boosters (Food & Drink)

GLP-1 is the satiety hormone that injectable drugs mimic. These foods/substances raise it naturally:

  • Yerba mate — Dr. Bikman's lab has published reports on this. Replaces coffee in his routine
  • Alulose — rare sugar, non-metabolisable, travels to intestines and significantly boosts GLP-1. Can be used as a sweetener in tea, coffee, or baking
  • Collagen peptides — also shown to increase GLP-1
  • Protein generally — moderate GLP-1 effect

GLP-1 Drugs (Ozempic, Mounjaro etc.)

GLP-1 drugs suppress appetite and slow gastric emptying — food can sit in the stomach for 24 hours instead of 4–6. They work, but diminishing returns set in and the consequences of stopping are significant.

What They Do Well

  • Significant appetite suppression
  • People naturally reduce carbohydrate intake
  • Substantial weight loss, especially for obese patients
  • Effective tool to break carb addiction during a coached programme

Concerns & Limitations

  • Sweet cravings return to baseline by 2 years on the drug
  • ~70% of people stop the drug within 2 years (constant nausea)
  • 40% of weight lost comes from lean mass (muscle and bone) — not just fat
  • Lost muscle may never return — particularly dangerous in older women
  • Weight regains rapidly on stopping
  • "Ozempic burps" — food sitting in stomach for 24 hours

Dr. Bikman's Recommended Approach if Using GLP-1 Drugs

  • Use a low dose (micro dose) — not the full weight loss dose
  • Cycle: 90 days on the drug with low-carb diet coaching simultaneously
  • Wean off after 90 days — assess if habits have stuck
  • If cravings return, cycle back on and try again
  • Results from Idaho clinic using this approach: vastly outperform drug-only trials — better fat loss AND better muscle retention
The goal of GLP-1 drugs in Dr. Bikman's view is to use them as a temporary tool to cure carbohydrate addiction — not as a lifelong medication. The drug makes it easier than ever to adopt low-carb habits, and the habits should eventually sustain without the drug.

Alcohol, Sleep & Stress

Three of the fastest triggers of insulin resistance: stress, poor sleep, and excessive carbohydrates. Alcohol and sleep deprivation both act through cortisol — elevating insulin the following day and making every calorie eaten more likely to be stored as fat.

Alcohol

  • People think alcohol helps sleep — they fall asleep faster but sleep quality is significantly worse throughout the night
  • Next morning: elevated cortisol, higher insulin resistance — every calorie consumed that day is more likely to be stored as fat
  • Alcohol is metabolically incompatible with fat loss goals
  • Cutting alcohol is one of the highest-impact single changes — with knock-on improvements to sleep, energy, and craving control

Sleep & Evening Glucose

  • Sleep deprivation is the leading cause of stress — and stress is a cardinal cause of insulin resistance
  • Going to bed with elevated or crashing blood glucose activates the sympathetic nervous system (fight-or-flight) — raises heart rate, raises body temperature, fragments sleep
  • A glucose spike before bed = poor sleep = elevated cortisol next day = higher insulin resistance = more fat storage that day
  • Evening is when people are weakest to cravings — structure the environment to prevent this rather than relying on willpower
Fix for sleepStop all eating after dinner. Ashwagandha before bed. Ice bath in the morning (resets body clock for better evening tiredness). No carb spike before bed.

Stress & Cortisol

  • Stress causes acute insulin resistance within hours — one of the fastest pathways to metabolic dysfunction
  • Ketones are anxiolytic — BHB directly reduces anxiety signalling in the brain. Being in ketosis makes stress management physiologically easier
  • Ashwagandha supplements cortisol suppression
  • Read books — Dr. Bikman's non-physical recommendation for 2026. Brain fuelled by ketones learns and retains better than on glucose

Testosterone & Men's Health

Men experience a gradual testosterone decline (andropause) — a less dramatic but real parallel to female menopause. Fatigue and weight gain in middle-aged men should prompt a testosterone check before assuming it's lifestyle alone.

  • If tired and gaining weight: get testosterone checked — it may be low and addressable
  • Losing weight raises testosterone — fat loss and testosterone are bidirectionally linked
  • Ice bath (cold plunge) + workout: some men have doubled testosterone levels with this combination
  • Supplements can support testosterone production before full TRT (testosterone replacement therapy) becomes necessary
How to testDoctor blood test — or home services (e.g. Blok, Joi in the US) that send kits by post Natural boostersIce bath + resistance workout combination. Zinc and magnesium sufficiency. Losing excess fat. Adequate sleep.

Women & Keto

Women are not small men — their hormonal cycle creates meaningful differences in how they respond to ketogenic diets. However, the concern that keto is uniquely stressful for women is overstated, according to human cortisol data.

Follicular Phase (Post-Period → Ovulation)

  • Low progesterone, rising estrogen
  • Women are fat-burning machines — free fatty acids 40–50% higher than men
  • Enter ketosis faster than men
  • Ideal time for stricter keto adherence

Luteal Phase (Post-Ovulation → Period)

  • High progesterone — a hunger hormone
  • Cravings significantly higher
  • Harder to stick to keto
  • Dr. Bikman: be generous with yourself during this phase
Dr. Isabella Cooper (UK) study: women switching between keto and high-carb diet showed no statistically significant change in cortisol at any point. The claim that keto is uniquely cortisol-stressful for women is not supported by the data.

Cancer & Metabolism

Dr. Thomas Seyfried's work (referenced extensively) proposes that cancer is primarily a metabolic disease of glucose and mitochondrial dysfunction — not a genetic disease of nuclear mutations. This is the Warburg Effect, modernised.

  • Cancer cells rely almost entirely on glucose as fuel (and glutamine secondarily)
  • PET scans inject radioactive glucose — cancer cells are visible because they absorb far more glucose than normal cells
  • Seyfried experiment: transplanting the nucleus of a cancer cell into a healthy cell produced a normal healthy cell. Transplanting the mitochondria of a cancer cell into a healthy cell produced a cancer cell — proving it is a mitochondrial, not nuclear, disease
  • Ketogenic diet starves cancer cells of glucose while normal cells thrive on ketones
  • Low-carb diets make chemotherapy significantly more effective by depriving cancer cells of their fuel
  • Dr. Bikman personally adheres to a ketogenic diet partly as cancer prevention — his mother died of cancer and first-degree relatives have elevated risk
This is an active and contested area of research. Dr. Seyfried's work is peer-reviewed. It does not claim keto cures cancer — it proposes metabolic deprivation as an adjunct to conventional treatment that makes those treatments more effective.

Fasting Insulin Test

Dr. Bikman's single most important policy wish: add insulin to all routine blood panels globally. Currently it is not routinely measured. Request it specifically.

Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM)

One of the most powerful behaviour-change tools available. People make changes of their own volition simply by seeing what happens in their blood in real time.

  • Costs ~£15–20 online, worn on the arm for 14 days
  • Shows glucose response to every food, drink, meal, and sleep pattern within minutes
  • Most revealing insight: how much poor sleep raises fasting glucose — often more than food choices
  • Stack with Whoop/HRV wearable: when HRV shows stress, cross-reference with CGM — often hypoglycaemia is the cause of what feels like stress or anxiety
