Visceral Fat

Visceral fat is metabolically active fat stored deep within the abdomen, surrounding organs. Unlike subcutaneous fat (pinchable), it is invisible from outside. You can be lean and still carry dangerous levels.

Visceral Fat → Insulin Resistance Cycle

Average visceral fat by age

Age 30Male: 1.2 lb · Female: 0.5 lb Age 40Male: 1.7 lb · Female: 0.7 lb Age 50Male: 2.2 lb · Female: 1.0 lb Age 60Male: 2.7 lb · Female: 1.54 lb

70% of women over 50 and 50% of men over 50 carry a high amount. From age 25–65, men see a 200% increase in visceral fat even if body weight stays the same.

Health risks

  • Doubles the risk of early mortality
  • 44% increased risk of metastatic cancer
  • Causes and worsens insulin resistance → type 2 diabetes
  • Chronic inflammation via cytokines → brain fog, fatigue, lethargy
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
  • Elevated cardiovascular disease risk

Causes & How to Reduce It

CAUSES

  • Ageing + hormonal decline (testosterone, oestrogen)
  • Sleep deprivation — 4 hrs/night for 2 weeks = 11% more visceral fat, no change on scales
  • Caloric excess from ultra-processed foods — just 5 days at +1,200–1,500 kcal caused measurable gain
  • Chronic stress (elevated cortisol)
  • Excess alcohol
  • Sedentary lifestyle

HOW TO REDUCE IT

  • Vigorous aerobic exercise — most effective type for visceral fat loss specifically
  • Intermittent fasting / caloric restriction
  • Prioritising sleep quality and duration
  • Reducing chronic stress
  • Cutting excess alcohol
Visceral fat is one of the first types to be lost in any weight-loss programme.

Measurement & Screening

Waist (women)>35 inches = warning sign Waist (men)>40 inches = warning sign DEXA scanMost direct measure. Target: <300 g visceral fat CGMContinuous glucose monitor — reveals insulin resistance indirectly

Insulin Resistance

Insulin resistance occurs when cells stop responding to insulin, preventing glucose from being absorbed. Think of insulin as a taxi driver — insulin resistance means the phone is ringing but nobody's picking up.

Normal vs. Insulin Resistant Glucose Flow

Consequences of Insulin Resistance

  • Blood glucose sits elevated → cellular damage
  • Pancreas overproduces insulin to compensate → eventually fails → type 2 diabetes
  • Energy crashes and hunger spikes 1 hour after meals
  • Brain insulin resistance → poor fat-storage signalling → more visceral fat

How to Improve Insulin Sensitivity

  • Vigorous aerobic exercise — muscles become glucose-responsive without needing insulin
  • Intermittent fasting and ketosis (metabolic switch)
  • Adequate sleep — sleep deprivation is a rapid driver of resistance
  • Reducing visceral fat (which directly blocks insulin signalling)

Fish Oil / Omega-3 (EPA & DHA)

90% of the US population and 80% globally are deficient. A low omega-3 index carries a similar mortality risk to smoking.

  • High omega-3 index → 5-year increased life expectancy
  • High omega-3 index → 66% lower Alzheimer's risk
  • Swiss study: only omega-3 (of omega-3, vitamin D, and resistance training) slowed epigenetic ageing independently
  • All three combined: slowed biological ageing 4 months in one year, 60% lower pre-frailty risk
Dose1.6–2 g EPA+DHA per day TargetOmega-3 index ≥ 8% StorageKeep in fridge; freeze bulk stock QualityTOTOX (total oxidation) score <10
Test first: Omega-3 index finger-prick test (~£20 online). You may be supplementing with a product that's not being absorbed or is oxidised — you won't know without testing.

Vitamin D3

D3 is the form produced by skin from sunlight. D2 (plant-based, found in mushrooms) is less effective. Vegans should seek D3 from lichen specifically.

  • Deficiency = accelerated biological ageing
  • Supplementing D3 in deficient people slowed biological ageing by ~2 years (large study)
  • No benefit if you are already sufficient — the goal is to avoid deficiency, not mega-dose
  • People with darker skin require far more sun exposure to produce equivalent D3 — supplementation more important
Supplement regulation: Some D3 supplements contain up to 10,000× the labelled dose. Third-party tested brands only (NSF or Consumer Lab verified).

Multivitamin

Fills micronutrient gaps caused by poor diet, depleted soils (glyphosate), and ultra-processed food. The COSMOS study using Centrum Silver is the key evidence cited.

  • Adults 65+ taking a daily multivitamin for 3 years reversed global brain ageing by 2.1 years
  • Reversed episodic memory ageing by almost 5 years
  • COSMOS study: slowed epigenetic ageing by several months after just 2 years
Key nutrientsVitamin C, D3, E, K, B vitamins, folate, selenium, zinc Men's noteDo NOT choose a multivitamin with added iron unless blood tests show deficiency

Magnesium

~50% of the population is deficient. Active people lose additional magnesium through sweat, increasing requirements further.

  • Required for 300+ enzymatic reactions
  • Critical for DNA repair from oxidative damage
  • Studies link adequate magnesium levels to lower cancer risk
  • Supports sleep quality
Target350–400 mg/day Food sourcesDark leafy greens, almonds

Creatine

Most well-studied supplement for muscle. Stored primarily in muscles, which are very greedy for it — saturation takes time.

  • Increases phosphocreatine in muscles → more training volume → greater growth
  • At 10 g/day: measurably increases brain creatine → reduces afternoon mental slump
  • At 20–25 g/day: negates sleep-deprivation cognitive effects and may temporarily raise performance above rested baseline
Muscle dose5 g/day Brain/cognitive dose10 g/day Sleep deprivation20–25 g/day (temporary) Saturation time3–4 weeks at 5 g/day from zero — no loading needed Side effectsBloating/nausea in some — halve dose and split with food
Gummies are useless: Consumer testing found almost no creatine gummies contain measurable creatine. Use creatine monohydrate powder, NSF certified only.

Urolithin A — Mitochondrial Rejuvenation

Mitophagy: Clearing Damaged Mitochondria
  • 20% life extension in mouse studies (preclinical)
  • Human muscle biopsies confirmed mitophagy activation
  • 1,000 mg/day in older adults: increased CD8+ T-cells and natural killer cells (immune rejuvenation)
  • +10% VO2 max vs. exercise alone (untrained); +5% in trained athletes
  • +10–12% hamstring strength in older adults vs. exercise alone
Dose1,000 mg/day AvailabilityOnline only — not available on the high street Budget alternativePomegranate juice pre-exercise — meta-analysis: up to 17% VO2 max improvement (only if you have the right gut bacteria)
50% of people lack the gut bacteria to convert pomegranate polyphenols into urolithin A — direct supplementation bypasses this lottery.

Curcumin (Phytosomal form only)

The active compound in turmeric. Standard curcumin is rapidly cleared by the liver (treated as a foreign substance). Phytosomal or liposomal delivery is required for cellular uptake.

  • Lowers TNF-alpha (major pro-ageing cytokine) by ~5 pg/mL — most potent natural compound known to do this
  • TNF-alpha inhibitor drugs: 50% lower Alzheimer's risk in rheumatoid arthritis patients
  • Anti-inflammatory without blunting exercise adaptations (unlike ibuprofen/NSAIDs)
Standard curcumin = largely wasted. Plain turmeric extract or non-phytosomal curcumin is cleared before it reaches cells. Seek phytosomal or liposomal form specifically.

Sulforaphane, Glutamine, Liposomal Glutathione & Iron

Sulforaphane (e.g. Avmacol by Nutramax)

  • Found in broccoli sprouts (100× more than mature broccoli)
  • Activates Phase 2 detox enzymes → converts BPA from fat-soluble to water-soluble → excreted in urine
  • 12 published clinical studies on the Avmacol brand

Glutamine

  • Amino acid used as energy by immune cells and gut cells
  • Reduced respiratory illness frequency in endurance athletes
  • Supports gut healing via alpha-ketoglutarate conversion

Liposomal Glutathione

  • Standard 'reduced glutathione' supplements cannot cross cell membranes — largely useless
  • Liposomal form encapsulates the molecule in a lipid layer that fuses with cell membranes
  • Intracellular antioxidant — reduces oxidation-driven brain ageing
Do not buy standard glutathione. Only liposomal form has demonstrated intracellular uptake.

Iron — Context Dependent

  • Most men should NOT supplement iron — excess free iron is oxidative and damages DNA
  • Pre-menopausal women: ~16% are deficient due to menstrual losses — may need it
  • Post-menopausal women: similar requirements to men — iron generally not needed
Always test iron levels before supplementing. Never assume deficiency.

Exogenous Ketones

Raise beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) in blood artificially, mimicking the fasted/ketogenic state. BHB is a signalling molecule as well as a fuel.

  • Increases GABA → calms background mental noise → improved focus
  • Raises BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) → learning and memory
  • Cognitive boost particularly useful for presentations, demanding interviews, heavy research
Fat-loss warning: Exogenous ketones suppress lipolysis (fat burning) for ~3 hours. If fasting for fat loss, do NOT take ketone supplements during the fast — you will briefly halt fat mobilisation.
Ketone IQUses 1,3-butanediol → converted to BHB in liver. Delayed onset. More affordable. Oxford KetoneBHB esterified — immediate + sustained effect. ~£30/dose.

Intermittent Fasting & Ketosis

Intermittent fasting reduces caloric intake without calorie counting. The real power beyond weight loss is the metabolic switch to ketosis and the activation of cellular repair.

The Metabolic Switch: Fed → Fasted → Ketosis

Dr. Patrick's Protocol

Protocol16:8 — 16 hrs fasted, 8-hr eating window Fasting windowMornings — extends overnight fast to reach ketosis Why morningGets into metabolic switch quickly; ketones provide morning cognitive boost

Benefits of the Metabolic Switch

  • Ketones provide brain with easily usable energy
  • BHB signals the brain to be cognitively sharp (evolutionary adaptation for food-seeking)
  • Increased GABA production → calmer, more focused, background anxiety reduced
  • Fasted state activates autophagy (cellular waste clearance) and mitophagy (mitochondrial clearance)
  • Body fat (including visceral fat) mobilised as fuel source

Fasted Exercise & Sleep/Eating Timing

Fasted Exercise

  • Aerobic endurance exercise done fasted produces better mitochondrial adaptations
  • Body becomes better at burning fat; continues doing so throughout the day
  • Short sessions appropriate fasted (e.g. 3 miles); sessions 10+ miles require fuel
  • Women: risk of hormonal disruption if large caloric deficit + high training volume + insufficient food — listen to your body

Eating Window & Sleep

  • Stop eating at least 3 hours before bed — eating activates sympathetic nervous system (fight-or-flight), fragmenting sleep
  • Light pre-bed snack if needed: cooled baked potato or green banana (resistant starch) — good for gut microbiome and actually improves sleep quality
  • Resistant starch: cook → cool → reheat (structure changes during cooling)

Vigorous = 70%+ max heart rate (jogging, running, cycling, swimming). HIIT = 80%+ MHR.

Exercise Snacks — Short Bursts Count

You don't need 30 continuous minutes. Short vigorous bursts throughout the day accumulate significant benefits:

  • Women doing 3.5 min/day vigorous activity → 40% lower cancer risk
  • Men and women accumulating 9 min/day of short vigorous bursts → 40% lower cancer mortality, 50% lower cardiovascular mortality
  • Body-weight squats, jumping jacks, high knees, playing tag — all count
  • Getting up every hour and doing 1 minute of vigorous movement breaks sedentary time and adds to your daily total
Recommendation: Replace "10,000 steps" goal with "10 minutes of getting your heart rate up per day." 10,000 steps is mostly light intensity and does not deliver the same mortality benefits.

Exercise & the Brain

  • Aerobic exercise is the single most effective intervention for brain health
  • Increases BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) — new neuron growth, strengthens connections
  • Improves both fluid intelligence (processing speed) and crystallised intelligence
  • 5 hours/week with HIIT = reverse heart ageing by 20 years (study cited)

Sedentary Behaviour — Independent Risk Factor

Sitting is an independent risk factor for disease even in people who exercise regularly. Particularly strongly linked to cancer.

  • Standing desks help; exercise snacks are better
  • Break sitting every hour with movement
  • Exercising for 1 hour then sitting for 8 hours does not fully offset the sedentary risk

Environmental Toxins

Three classes of endocrine disrupting chemicals (EDCs) dominate: BPA, phthalates, and PFAS (forever chemicals). All interfere with hormone function — primarily sex hormones and thyroid hormone.

Three Main Endocrine Disruptors — Sources & Targets

BPA — Detailed Health Effects & Mitigation

  • Acts as oestrogen mimetic AND androgen receptor binder — disrupts both male and female hormones
  • Adolescent boys with highest BPA had 50% lower testosterone than those with lowest levels
  • Linked to low sperm count, poor motility, and abnormal morphology
  • Pregnant women with high BPA: 6× more likely to have a child with autism spectrum disorder
  • 'BPA-free' plastics often contain BPS — equally or more harmful

Mitigation

  • Request digital receipts instead of paper — cashiers handling receipts have extremely high BPA levels
  • Cashiers: wear nitrile gloves (not latex — latex does not block BPA)
  • Hand lotion or sanitiser while handling receipts increases absorption ~100-fold
  • Store acidic foods (ketchup, hot sauce, vinegar) in glass — acidity dramatically accelerates leaching
  • Avoid canned soup especially — increases BPA levels by ~1,000%
  • Supplement sulforaphane to boost BPA excretion via urine

Kitchen Audit — Changes Ranked by Impact

  • Replace Teflon pans with stainless steel, cast iron, or ceramic — largest single domestic PFAS source
  • Remove all black plastic containers and utensils — recycled electronics contain cancer-causing brominated flame retardants
  • Store acidic condiments in glass — ketchup, hot sauce, mustard, vinegar leach rapidly from plastic
  • Use a reverse osmosis water filter — removes microplastics, BPA, phthalates (also removes trace minerals — compensate with multivitamin)
  • Stainless steel blender jug — plastic blenders shed high levels of microplastics via friction
  • Glass water over plastic — plastic has nanoplastics that enter the bloodstream; glass has larger particles that pass through
  • Wooden or stainless utensils — silicone often contains plastic; black plastic spatulas are worst
  • Glass or bamboo-lidded glass containers for food storage and takeaway

Hormones & Testosterone

Testosterone levels in men have dropped by up to 20% over the last two decades — with environmental endocrine disruptors considered the primary population-level cause.

Male Testosterone Decline

  • Declines ~1% per year from age 30
  • Age 25–65: 200% increase in visceral fat even with no change in body weight
  • Testosterone helps burn visceral fat — as it declines, net visceral fat accumulation accelerates

Contributing factors to population-level decline

  • Environmental EDCs — BPA, phthalates, PFAS (considered primary driver)
  • Sleep deprivation — one of the most impactful individual levers
  • Excess refined sugar in diet
  • Micronutrient deficiencies — especially zinc and magnesium (essential for testosterone synthesis)

Female Hormones, Perimenopause & Visceral Fat

  • Oestrogen tells the body to store fat subcutaneously (thighs, buttocks) rather than viscerally
  • As oestrogen declines in perimenopause, fat shifts sharply to visceral storage — often feels overnight
  • Perimenopause typically starts mid-40s — 8–10% annual visceral fat increase begins here
  • SWAN study: accelerated visceral fat gain starts 2 years before the final menstrual period
  • Obesity accelerates ovarian ageing → earlier menopause
  • High PFAS exposure → menopause 1–2 years earlier
  • Testosterone (even in women) helps burn visceral fat — some consider testosterone therapy during perimenopause for this reason

Sleep

Sleep is one of the fastest levers for accumulating visceral fat, impairing immune function, disrupting glucose metabolism, and dropping testosterone. It is undervalued relative to diet and exercise.

  • 4 hrs/night for 2 weeks = 11% more visceral fat — no change on the scales
  • Accelerates immune system ageing significantly
  • Directly impairs blood glucose regulation and insulin sensitivity
  • Testosterone drops significantly with inadequate sleep
  • Stop eating at least 3 hours before bed — activates sympathetic nervous system, fragments sleep quality
  • Continuous glucose monitors reveal sleep's direct effect on metabolic health in real time
  • Melatonin supplements are frequently mis-dosed at manufacturing level — third-party tested brands only

Peak Span

A new concept from Duke University: maintaining within 90% of your peak physiological function for as long as possible. Beyond health span (disease-free) — this is about staying near peak performance.

Physiological Function Over Lifespan — When Systems Peak & Decline

When Systems Peak

Female reproductivePeaks ~25, sharp decline to ~40, effectively ends ~50–52 Immune functionPeaks ~18–25, steady decline thereafter Muscle mass / bone densityPeaks ~25, steady decline Fluid intelligencePeaks ~20–25 (processing speed — answering without prior knowledge) Crystallised intelligencePeaks ~40–45 (accumulated knowledge, wisdom, pattern recognition) VO2 max / cardiorespiratoryPeaks ~20–25
Crystallised intelligence peaking at 40–45 makes midlife the prime time for entrepreneurship, strategic problem-solving, and leadership roles — you have maximum accumulated knowledge plus still adequate processing speed.

How to Maintain Peak Span

  • Vigorous aerobic exercise — cardiorespiratory fitness, brain, immune function, visceral fat
  • Resistance training — muscle mass and bone density
  • Omega-3 supplementation — biological ageing rate
  • Sleep — immune system ageing specifically
  • Novel cognitive engagement — fluid and crystallised intelligence
  • Avoiding endocrine disruptors — hormonal and ovarian health
  • Intermittent fasting — cellular repair, metabolic health

GLP-1 Drugs

GLP-1 receptor agonists (Ozempic/Wegovy = semaglutide; Mounjaro = tirzepatide) suppress appetite and slow gastric emptying, mimicking caloric restriction. Powerful for obesity — controversial for mild overweight use.

Benefits (for appropriate candidates)

  • Significant weight and visceral fat loss
  • Cardiovascular, cancer, and Alzheimer's risk all fall with weight reduction
  • Life-changing for those with obesity who cannot lose weight by lifestyle alone
  • Lowest-dose Ozempic can match intermittent fasting for 5–10% body weight loss

Risks & Concerns

  • Weight regain when stopping — appetite returns more intensely than before
  • Up to 40% of weight lost can be muscle if protein + resistance training not maintained
  • Bone loss (mechanism unclear)
  • Increased kidney cancer signal
  • Black-box warning for thyroid cancer (animal data only)
  • Gallstone formation — some require gallbladder removal
  • Nausea and GI upset

Key Practical Points

  • May need to be taken for life to maintain results — most regain all weight within ~1 year of stopping
  • If stopping, taper dose gradually rather than stopping abruptly — slows rate of weight regain
  • Maintain protein intake and resistance training throughout to prevent muscle loss
  • Dr. Patrick's concern: widespread use by people wanting to lose 10–15 lb who could achieve this through lifestyle changes — long-term effects in healthy-weight individuals unknown
The no-free-lunch reality: These drugs make you not feel hungry, which mimics caloric restriction. When you stop, the body's hunger response rebounds — often described as a ferocious, animalistic urge to eat far more intense than before starting the medication.

Finger-prick blood spot test, ordered online, done at home. Measures the % of omega-3 fatty acids in red blood cell membranes.

Brain Ageing & Alzheimer's Risk

Multiple lifestyle, supplement, and cognitive factors converge on brain ageing. Novel learning and aerobic exercise are the two highest-impact modifiable levers.

  • Novel cognitive engagement → strong Alzheimer's protection (London taxi drivers: 25,000 streets memorised, negligible Alzheimer's rates, physically larger hippocampi)
  • Learning a new language → rapid decrease in Alzheimer's risk
  • High omega-3 index → 66% lower dementia risk
  • Curcumin (phytosomal) → lowers TNF-alpha → linked to 50% lower Alzheimer's risk (via TNF-alpha inhibition pathway)
  • Aerobic exercise → BDNF → new neuron growth, stronger connections, greater brain plasticity
  • Sleep → brain waste clearance and neurological repair — inadequate sleep is a direct Alzheimer's risk driver
  • Vitamin D3 deficiency → accelerated brain ageing
  • Multivitamin (3 years in adults 65+) → reversed brain ageing 2.1 yrs overall, 5 yrs episodic memory
  • Retirement without cognitive engagement → rapid cognitive decline and earlier dementia onset
  • AI over-reliance → EEG studies show brain connectivity nearly halved when thinking is outsourced vs. writing manually; 83% of AI users couldn't recall details of text written with AI assistance
  • Handwriting notes (vs. typing or AI) significantly improves memory retention and cognitive encoding
